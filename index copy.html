Navient Data

By: Holliday Woodard



Using The Consumer Financial Protection Bureau, I developed infographics and analyzed the student loan complaints over the past three years (2021-2023). I first focused my analysis to New Jersey, my home state. I filtered the data so it showed me the results for complaints in getting a loan as a student. It showed that there were four complaints over three years in New Jersey. States around New Jersey like Connecticut and Delaware had even fewer complaints. They only recieved one complaint over three years from students about getting a loan.

navient5.pdf
Infogram

I then kept all of the same filters but changed the complaint to "struggling to repay your loan." I found this caused a major increase in number of complaints recieved. Now New Jersey had 38 complaints , Maryland had 30 and Virginia had 37. So while students in these states are able to get a loan without resistance for the most part, they are much worse in repaying back the money they borrowed.

While examining both charts I noticed that the countries in the midwest and northwest such as North Dakota, South Dakota, Wyoming and Nebraska recieved little to no complaints for either filter: getting a loan and struggling to repay the loan. This may mean that there is a fewer rate of people in these states requesting loans and therefore less poeple to have to repay them.
